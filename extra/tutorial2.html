Theoretical

  1. Show that across a stationary shock wave,

e2−e1=p1+p22(u1−u2).

This is called as the Hugoniot condition and it is satisfied even across a moving shock wave.

  1. For a perfect gas across a normal shock wave, show that

T2T1=p2p1[γ+1γ−1+p2p11+γ+1γ−1p2p1].

  1. What is the upper bound on the induced velocity (V2) before a moving normal shock?

  2. Find an expression for the maximum induced Mach number (M2) before a moving normal shock?

  3. For a normal shock, show that

M22=2+(γ−1)M122γM12−(γ−1)

p2p1=1+2γγ+1(M12−1)

ρ2ρ1=(γ+1)M12(γ−1)M12+2

T2T1=2+(γ−1)M122+(γ−1)M22

  1. Given two stations (A and B) in a one dimensional adiabatic duct, show that aA∗=aB∗ even if the flow in-between the stations is not in equilibrium.

  2. Show that T02T01=1 for a moving normal shock in the shock reference frame.

  3. If θ=f(M1,β) for an oblique shock, what is f ? Here, M1 is the upstream Mach number, θ is the flow deflection angle and β is the shock angle.

  4. Show that the pressure difference across a oblique shock wave with wave angle β may be expressed in the form

p2−p11/2ρ1u12=4γ+1(sin2⁡β−1M12)
