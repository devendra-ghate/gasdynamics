Gas Constant

The universal gas constant for an ideal gas is Ru=8.3145 J/mol/K and

pV=nRuT=(m/M)RuT

where, n is the number of moles of the gas. Also, m is the mass of the gas and M is the molar mass of the gas.

This gives us

p(V/m)=(Ru/M)T∴pv=RT

where R=Ru/M is the gas specific constant. This is a mass specific constant.

On the other hand,

p(V/n)=RuT∴pv^=R^T

where v^ is the mole specific volume and R^=Ru is the mole specific gas constant.

In the question 11, the ratio R/R^=(Ru/M)/Ru=1/M.
